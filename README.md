# Performence Booster for Students 

Final project for the Building AI course

## Summary
Many students today struggle to achieve satisfactory exam marks, yet only a small percentage ever truly understand where they went wrong. Often, students are left wondering which specific topics caused them to lose points, but these insights are rarely provided. I want to build an AI that analyzes exam sheets to tell a student exactly where their answer lacked depth, precision, or accuracy. By training this AI on exam sheets from various schools, it will learn to identify common pitfalls and recurring weak points across subjects like Math and Physics, finally helping students acknowledge and correct their mistakes.



## Background
I have always wanted to know exactly where I lost marks. I knew I was losing points in History, but I couldn't pinpoint why. After examining my exam papers multiple times, I realized that I perform top-class on conceptual questions, but I frequently trip up on memory-based details like dates and specific facts. Once I identified this pattern, I shifted my study strategy to focus more on memorizing facts, knowing that my conceptual understanding was already strong. That change helped me improve my grade from a B to an A+ in History. I believe every student needs a tool like this; as humans, we all make mistakes and miss critical details. Since AI is excellent at precision, it is the perfect tool to help students identify the specific gaps in their knowledge that they might otherwise overlook.

## Data sources and AI methods
The project will require a dataset of anonymized exam papers (handwritten and typed) paired with their corresponding teacher-graded rubrics and feedback. This data would need to be collected from volunteer students and teachers, ensuring all personal identifiable information (PII) is removed. the AI will use OCR to read the characters and identify them and use pattern recognition to recognizea mistake and the categorize it as one

## Challenges

Handwriting Variability: AI often struggles to accurately read messy or unique handwriting, which could lead to incorrect feedback.

Subjectivity: Different teachers grade the same question differently; the AI might struggle to understand the "nuance" of partial credit.

Ethical Considerations: Privacy is paramount. We must ensure student data is fully anonymized and never used to track or penalize students in their school records.

Scope: This project does not replace human teachers. It cannot grade subjective essays as accurately as a human, nor can it provide the emotional encouragement a mentor offers.
